Circles

Circles are Lean In’s bread and butter. In groups of 10-12 individuals, we hold weekly meetings that facilitate the personal and professional growth of our members. Our circles are led by our Lean In trained circle moderators (interested in becoming a moderator? Find out how here). Every one of our circles is built on the foundation of three aims: to teach skills, spark dialogue, and build community. 

Circles come in two flavors: general and special interest. General circles are best for those who want to meet a diverse group of individuals from across disciplines. Special interest circles, meanwhile, provide community in the common interests and experiences of their members.

Three Pillars

Skill-building

 Learn useful and implementable skills

Professional tips from a CCE counselor
Cover letter, interview, and resume writing practice
Techniques for energizing and boosting productivity

Dialogue

Challenge yourself to intense conversations and self-reflection

Introspection and sharing with members
Examining how to make the most out of your time and aligning your commitments with your passions and values
Reflection on how diversity shapes experience

Community 

Thrive in the support and inspiration of your Lean In peers

Benefiting from mutual peer mentorship
Weekly goal setting
Bonding through 1:1 chats, circle outings, and social movie/game nights

Join a Circle

This semester we are holding 6 circles, and, in addition, one moderator training circle led by co-presidents Serina and Kara. Circle sign-ups will open at the beginning of October, but you can get in touch with our mods before then by shooting any of us an email and/or attending our recruitment events in September.

  1. College Bootcamp (General) -   Are you new to college and are struggling to balance everything? Or are you a rising senior still seeking the perfect balance between school, free time and your job search? Regardless of where you are on the spectrum of age or experience, our College Bootcamp circle is the perfect college cheat sheet for anyone unsure of the specific career path they would like to pursue but certain they would appreciate the support and structure that Lean In offers. Our circle helps introduce our members to a variety of disciplines while also providing tips to thrive in the classroom and beyond. Moderators: Lauren Winters & Crystal Lee
  2. Lean In & Out (Special Interest) - As a minority and especially a double minority -- queer and women-identifying or non-cis -- leaning in can require extra strength, calling for not only personal courage but also external support. We propose a space that cultivates both, that isn’t afraid to tackle challenging subjects and goals but understands at the same the realities of challenge and fear. We hope that through the dialogue, skills, and community that we develop in this circle as well as by reaching out to LGBTQ individuals working in industry, we will begin to build the muscle together to address those realities. At the intersection of actionability and acceptance, we will learn to lean in the way we want to by leaning on each other first. Moderators: Serina Chang & Jane Wu
  3. Reorientation: Looking at Columbia from A Different Perspective (General) - Reorientation seeks to offer another way to define, approach, and achieve success that places mental and physical health as a priority. The circle will offer members a support system that focuses on meaningful personal relationships and practical approaches to thriving together at Columbia and beyond, both in our professional and personal life. We believe success, however one defines it, does not have to come at the cost of wellness; in fact, success will come as the result of prioritizing joy and health. Moderators: Alice Wu & Livy Tang
  4. Hello Worlds (Special Interest) - Hello Worlds is for individuals who hold interests in fields so often set in opposition that they can feel like two different planets: STEM and the humanities. Circle meetings will center on the challenges, both professional and personal, that come with being interdisciplinary. Through discussion of our interests and professional struggles, the circle will serve as a space to learn practical strategies for carving career paths that fit our interests and to learn about the amazing interests of other members. Moderator: Kara Schechtman
  5. The Dinner Party - You are invited to the Dinner Party, a supportive space to share food, look at art, and discuss ideas about gender identity. This circle is for anyone; experience in the arts is not necessary. We will use artworks and feminist texts as vehicles for exploring who we are and who we could be. Ultimately, the dinner party circle is about calibrating your self-identity in ways that support the decisions, desires, and goals you have. Moderators: Runy & Lewei
  6. Into The Deep End - If you want to make a splash, you have to jump into the deep end. The same applies to our lives in college, where we often find ourselves swimming in the shallow end by doing what’s safe: hanging out with the people we’re most comfortable with, taking the subjects we’re good at, and progressing down paths well-trodden by similar high achievers. I am establishing this circle at Columbia and at the University of Sydney for young women who want to shatter their professional, academic, and social limitations by stepping out of their comfort zones. Each week, circle members will need to come together to accomplish one item on their bucket list (ie: “truly help a stranger”, “befriend someone you normally wouldn’t befriend”) and will convene later in the week for an in-depth discussion. Moderator: Rachel Wu (Abroad)

Become a Moderator

Moderators, who are also board members of Lean In, lead our weekly circle meetings (either alone or with a co-moderator). Moderators also play a part in planning Lean In events and initiatives.

All moderators must complete a semester of training led by the presidents of Lean In. The training session, which meets once weekly, covers the topics of the Lean In Core Curriculum with each member leading one meeting. Moderators then receive valuable feedback from other moderators-in-training and the presidents.

By participating in moderator training for Lean In, you get the rare opportunity to receive feedback and training on your leadership skills. And, once you’re done with training, the experience of leading a circle is rewarding and fulfilling.

If you are interested in becoming a moderator, please complete this application for Fall 2017 moderator training. We generally train up to ten moderators a semester.

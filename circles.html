Circles

Circles are Lean Inâ€™s bread and butter. In groups of 10-12 individuals, we hold weekly meetings that facilitate the personal and professional growth of our members. Our circles are led by our Lean In trained circle moderators (interested in becoming a moderator? Find out how here). Every one of our circles is built on the foundation of three aims: to teach skills, spark dialogue, and build community. 

Circles come in two flavors: general and special interest. General circles are best for those who want to meet a diverse group of individuals from across disciplines. Special interest circles, meanwhile, provide community in the common interests and experiences of their members.

Three Pillars

Skill-building

 Learn useful and implementable skills

Professional tips from a CCE counselor
Cover letter, interview, and resume writing practice
Techniques for energizing and boosting productivity

Dialogue

Challenge yourself to intense conversations and self-reflection

Introspection and sharing with members
Examining how to make the most out of your time and aligning your commitments with your passions and values
Reflection on how diversity shapes experience

Community 

Thrive in the support and inspiration of your Lean In peers

Benefiting from mutual peer mentorship
Weekly goal setting
Bonding through 1:1 chats, circle outings, and social movie/game nights

Join a Circle

This semester we are holding 7 circles, and, in addition, one moderator training circle led by co-presidents Serina and Kara. Circle sign ups are closed for the Spring, but check back in the Fall to join.

  1. Step Out - To go beyond leaning into current structures of inequality, the goal of this circle is to Step Out, to provide an open minded and supportive setting for members to critically rethink systems of privilege. Step Out aims to help its members (highly educated Columbia women with bright career prospects) to overcome their nervousness around their own privilege; members can find personal and actionable ways to use their privilege as a sword to bring about real positive change. Moderator: Runyang
  2. Future Leaders (Tech) - The most compelling personal stories are the ones that demonstrate your values and motivations while illustrating how you overcame hardship. Our circle aims to help women in technology create their compelling personal stories by guiding them in identifying their adversities and turning these into opportunities.  Moderators: Arunima & Rachel
  3. Diversity - We want to reiterate that 2018 is the year of women and in turn use that platform to speak to how diversity is intertwined with strength and empowerment. We would like to focus on three specific areas to help relate the idea: learning how we define ourselves, how we define others, and how others define us. Moderators: Athena & Malavika
  4. Headspace - We want to make sure that Columbia students, when navigating the stresses of day-to-day life, are paying attention to their own personal happiness. Our circle will prompt members to think through what happiness means to them and develop wellness techniques that will make their own personal happiness, however they conceive of it, a priority in their life. Moderators: Lewei & Kara 
  5. Changing Tides: Female Representation in Media - We focus on emerging and established forms of media like film, publication and television to critique the depiction of the role of women in these mediums. By highlighting the shifts and dynamism in female representation in media through objective critiquing, we aim to discuss the challenges and accomplishments in this field and take away insights that can inform our attempts to empower us and our community. Moderators: Sombul & Daya
  6. Lean In Faith - We seek to provide members with an open space to address both how religion affects gender roles in and out of the workplace and how, additionally, gender can affect religious identities. We will strive to create dialogue with people of different religious beliefs to look at the effects of faith on each womanâ€™s modern day approach to religion and intersectional identities. Moderators: Crystal & Emma
  7. #Taboo- In #taboo, we will tackle the cultural and social attitudes towards the topics of sex, sexual identity, relationships and love. In this circle, no romantic or sexual topic will be taboo, allowing us to explore the impact society has on sex and love, as we strive to be informed activists in these areas.  Moderators: Colette & Louisa

Become a Moderator

Moderators, who are also board members of Lean In, lead our weekly circle meetings (either alone or with a co-moderator). Moderators also play a part in planning Lean In events and initiatives.

All moderators must complete a semester of training led by the presidents of Lean In. The training session, which meets once weekly, covers the topics of the Lean In Core Curriculum with each member leading one meeting. Moderators then receive valuable feedback from other moderators-in-training and the presidents.

By participating in moderator training for Lean In, you get the rare opportunity to receive feedback and training on your leadership skills. And, once youâ€™re done with training, the experience of leading a circle is rewarding and fulfilling.
